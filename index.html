Background

How Did It Start?

The idea of the #BlackLivesMatter movement was formed by George Floyd’s death on May 25,2020 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. George Floyd was a black man, who was wrongfully murdered by a cop who knelt on his neck for about 9 minutes, without caring about Floyd’s pleadings. George Floyd stated that he couldn’t breath, and that he was hurting, and even claimed that the cop was gonna kill him. “Please I can’t breath. My stomach hurts. My neck hurts. Everything hurts. They’re going to kill me.”, this was all said by Floyd, in the last minutes of his life, while the cop kept his knee on his neck. Along with this cop, there’s 3 more who took part in George Floyd’s death, who have now been charged for their aiding.
This tragic loss started out with a staff in the store Cup Foods, who called the police because they suspected that George Floyd wanted to counterfeit a $20 bill. It has been allegedly said that Minneapolis has had counterfeit bills roaming Cup Foods neighborhoods. Multiple stores around Cup Foods have claimed to never have had a problem with counterfeit bills before George Floyd’s death. So far public officials haven’t contributed to the location of George Floyd’s $20 bill. Therefore it can’t be confirmed if the accusations towards George Floyd were actually right.

Aftermath

George Floyd’s death started the #BlackLivesMatter movement, which has now escalated. This movement has encouraged all types of races and backgrounds to speak up about the injustice black people get. This has sparked many emotions among the U.S residents, who have now made many petitions towards this cause, along with multiple places to donate, and protest all over the 50 states. Celebrities and influencers of all types, have used their platform to contribute and support this movement. Many of these celebrities and influencers have donated and have gone to protests to support the #BlackLivesMatter movement. Along with them there are thousands protesting for equality and justice.
Although the protests are for a great cause, some have made the protests into non-peaceful ones. Through some states there have been people burning small businesses, robbing, and even going as far as burning an American flag. A police precinct in Minneapolis was set on fire within the third day of protesting after Floyd’s death. In Providence, Rhode Island a protest turned into a dangerous riot when people burned a police cruiser, and broke into Providence Place Mall with around 300 people and looted the shops there.

How To Help

What's Some Ways To Help?

For weeks now people have put on their mask and gone out to protest for the #BlackLivesMatter movement all over the U.S. Protests have been a great way to get people’s voices heard, many have made posters along with videos to spread awareness of this. These videos have not only shown the worst from this, like the burnings and the police brutality, but also unity among all the people coming together to support this important cause. Along with protests there’s many more ways to help. People have organized a bunch of petitions to support #BlackLivesMatter movement in which thousands have signed. Another way of helping without going out is texting certain numbers which would help certain causes for this movement. Donating is also a great way to help this movement by supporting #BlackLivesMatter. Last but not least, a great way to help is by speaking up and raising awareness to this problem.
